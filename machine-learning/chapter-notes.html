2.2 Assessing Model Accuracy

Many models will be introduced. This is necessary since there is no model that is best in all situations.

2.2.1 Measuting the quality of fit

In regression setting the main measure is mean square error. Given a set of training data we can calculate the training MSE. But you can always build a model where the MSE is 0. The MSE often is not of intrinsic interest.

What we really want to a measure of the models ability to predict new data. Let ($x_0$, $y_0$) be a pair of data points not seen in the training data. Then our quality of fit predictor can be:

$$ S = Ave(f(x_0) - y_0) $$

As the number of parameters increases, training MSE will fall to 0. test MSE, however, will reach a minimum and then increase.
